CLOCK MODULE

The clock refers to the microchip that regulates the timing and speed of all computer functions. It is used to synchronize all computer components of the motherboard. Within the chip is a crystal or a timer that vibrates or outputs pulses at a specific frequency when electricity is applied. The shortest time any computer is capable of performing is one clock or one vibration or one pulse of the lok module. The speed of a computer processor is measured in clock speed, for example, 1 Mhz is one million cycles, or vibrations, a second.
